Interview Overview

Each hour-long episode of The Genesis Science Report features two remote expert interviews, each lasting approximately 13 minutes. These segments typically highlight a recent scientific publication that demonstrates the harmony between observable science and the biblical account of Creation.

You will receive a Zoom meeting link via email the day before the interview. Due to the fast-paced nature of filming and David’s tight schedule, it is essential that you join the Zoom call 15 minutes prior to your scheduled interview time. This allows the production team to ensure your audio, video, and connection are properly set up.

The format of this interview is a dynamic conversation, similar to news-style programming. It is not a lecture or presentation. The studio team, including the Producer and Technical Director, will decide when and how to incorporate provided visuals.

Technical Requirements

Internet Speed: A stable internet connection is crucial for a smooth Zoom interview.
  • Please ensure your internet speed is sufficient to avoid any disruptions.
  • You can test your internet speed by googling "internet speed test" or visiting fast.com.
  • The minimum recommended speed is 10Mbps.
Camera/Video: A high-resolution external webcam is recommended to ensure high-quality video.
  • A high-quality external webcam will produce a much clearer video image than a built-in laptop camera.
    • Recommended options
    • Logitech C920 HD Pro Webcam
    • Anker PowerConf C200 2K Webcam
  • Position your webcam at eye level, or slightly above, for a professional look.
  • Good lighting is key, so ensure your workspace is well-lit from the front (light shining straight toward your face) and try to avoid shadows.
  • Check your background to make sure it looks clean, uncluttered, and professional. 
  • Please, unless absolutely necessary, do NOT use the “blurred background” settings in Zoom apps.
Microphone/Audio: Clear audio is essential for a professional broadcast.
  • We recommend using a high-quality microphone (USB microphones are ideal). 
    Recommended options:
    • Shure MV7X Podcast Microphone
    • Logitech Blue Yeti USB Microphone
  • If possible, avoid using built-in laptop microphones as they tend to pick up background noise.
  • Test your microphone setup before the interview to ensure optimal audio quality.
  • We're happy to provide recommendations if needed.

 

Production Etiquette

    Camera Composition: Proper camera framing is crucial for a professional on-screen appearance. Maintain only a small amount of headroom above your head, keeping your head centered from side to side, and in the top third of the frame from top to bottom.   • Timing: GSR airs on broadcast television, thus hitting a hard-timed deadline is a REQUIREMENT for every production. We wish to always have a guest viewed in the best light possible, we never want to interrupt a guest due to time constraints. To avoid this, please shorten your answers as the interview nears the 10-minute mark. David will attempt to give you cues if possible, but if we are 2 minutes from the hard break and you speak for 2 minutes, then David will have to interrupt you and simply say “Thanks for your thoughts, and we’ll be back after the break.” to avoid you being cut mid-sentence.

  • Attire: The recommended attire for guests is business casual at a minimum. 
    • Think broadcast news, although a tie is not necessary. 
    • David will be wearing a sportcoat and dress shirt without a tie.
    • Avoid clothing with busy or distracting patterns. 
    • Avoid wearing hats. 

  • Simplify Your Topic: If a topic is particularly complex due to its specialized scientific nature in a certain field, we always encourage you to simplify it, give analogies, and use stories. You will receive more positive feedback when the majority of the viewing audience can grasp the message being presented.

Interview Flow

  1. Joining the Zoom Call: 15 minutes prior to your interview, you will use the zoom link in your inbox to join the meeting. The producer will admit you to the zoom call, brief you on how the interview will flow, and provide instructions for adjusting your lighting and camera composition.

  2. Segment Start and Introduction: Once we have worked through any technical hiccups, the screen will go black, and will remain black until after the intro sequence plays out of the last commercial break. David will welcome the audience back from the commercial break, briefly introduce the interview topic, and introduce you as our expert guest.

  3. Interview: The conversation will last approximately 10-13 minutes. The production team controls the timing and any visual elements (images or videos you provided). (See Visuals and Graphics section for more detail).

  4. Closing Remarks: David will begin to wrap the conversation up and might ask you for a closing remark. During this closing time please keep your answers short (within 30seconds), so we avoid a situation where you have to be cut off for time. With about one minute left, David will wrap up the conversation, thank you for appearing on the show, and close out the segment.

  5. Outro and Credits: After David has closed the segment, there will be around 1 minute of ending graphics/credits that we will cycle through before fading to black.
